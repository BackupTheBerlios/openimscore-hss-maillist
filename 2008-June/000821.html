Because SIP is the core signalling protocol in IMS, it's not really 
designed to get the IP-address and use it for something. I think you're 
better of getting the IMPU of the client (e.g., from REGISTER-requests 
as Franz Edler explained or by other means) and then establish a session 
with it (i.e., an INVITE-request). The SDP-answer from the client should 
include the IP-address to be used. This supports clients with more then 
one IP-address, such as in Fixed-Mobile Converged scenarios - the 
IP-address in the Contact-header is the IP-address that the client uses 
for signaling.

PS: If you explain what you're gonna use the IP-address then maybe 
somebody can come up with a different solution...
