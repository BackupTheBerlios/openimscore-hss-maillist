Call for Presentations: Globecom 2007 D&D Forum on IMS

Deadline Extended to August 15, 2007

 

The IEEE Globecom 2007 will include a Design & Developers (D&D) Forum
consisting of a collection of topics of interest to telecommunications
practitioners. Each D&D Forum session will comprise a series of
presentations relating to one topic.  IP Multimedia Subsystem (IMS) will
be a featured session at the IEEE Globecom D&D Forum. For more
information, see http://www.ieee-globecom.org/dd_forum.html 

The session on IMS will address the state of the art in IMS. Emphasis
will be on practical and commercial aspects of IMS.  Presentations in
following areas are solicited:

*	IMS-based applications 
*	The role of IMS in network access convergence 
*	IMS standardization including A-IMS
*	IMS-based secure communications
*	Migration strategies 
*	Interoperability and interworking issues
*	IMS market and deployment status 
*	IMS clients and devices
*	Other related areas
*	IMS open source and testbed implementations (enough proposals
received - no more accepted).

The session will consist of short power point presentations of 20 - 30
minutes. The speakers will be selected from a broad spectrum of
expertise including equipment vendors, telecom operators, application
developers, and academia. 

Please submit an abstract (about 250 words) of the presentation along
with a short bio of the speaker by August 15, 2007 to
vvarma at telcordia.com with a copy to mloushin at telcordia.com and
jafried at ieee.org. Speakers will be notified of acceptance by August 20,
2007. Full presentations are due before the conference for posting at
the conference website.
