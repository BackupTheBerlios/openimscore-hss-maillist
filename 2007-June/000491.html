Call for Presentations: Globecom 2007 D&D Forum on IMS

 

The IEEE Globecom 2007 will include a Design & Developers (D&D) Forum
consisting of a collection of topics of interest to telecommunications
practitioners. Each D&D Forum session will comprise a series of
presentations relating to one topic.  IP Multimedia Subsystem (IMS) will
be a featured session at the IEEE Globecom D&D Forum. For more
information, see http://www.ieee-globecom.org/DDaccesssub.html.

 

The session on IMS will address the state of the art in IMS including,
but not limited to, IMS core network issues, IMS applications, role of
IMS in network access convergence, IMS standardization, A-IMS, migration
strategies, IMS open source and testbed implementations,
interoperability and interworking issues, and IMS market and deployment
status. Emphasis will be on practical and commercial aspects of IMS.

 

The session will consist of short power point presentations of 20 - 30
minutes. The speakers will be selected from a broad spectrum of
expertise including equipment vendors, telecom operators, application
developers, and academia. 

 

Please submit an abstract of the presentation along with a short bio of
the speaker by June 20, 2007 to vvarma at telcordia.com with a copy to
mloushin at telcordia.com and jafried at ieee.org. Speakers will be notified
of acceptance by July 1, 2007. Full presentations are due September 1,
2007 for posting at the conference website.
